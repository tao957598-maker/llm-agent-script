# OKP
## One-stop KYC Platform and Phase 1B Overview

### Background
- KYC is a significant element in the fight against financial crime and money laundering. (Page5)
- Hong Kong enacted the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance (AMLO), CAP. 615 on March 1, 2018, making KYC and CDD a statutory requirement for DNFBP and registered TCSP. (Page5)
- Implementation of Customer Due Diligence (CDD) and KYC processes for DNFBPs and TCSPs (Page6)
- Screening for potential risks related to national security, criminal activities, and political exposure (Page6)
- Seamless integration of essential checks and reviews (Page6)

### Description
- The document outlines the specifications for Phase 1B of the OKP project, building upon the foundational work completed in Phase 1A. (Page5)
- Phase 1B focuses on enhancing the platform's capabilities to further support KYC, anti-money laundering, countering the financing of terrorism, anti-bribery and corruption, and associated legislation. (Page5)
- The key development is the introduction of an all-in-one system that integrates essential KYC functions into a unified platform, offering enhanced features for NSS, Company search, Transaction Review, and Customer Profile. (Page5)
- Building upon the foundation established in Phase 1A, Phase 1B extends the platform's functionality to meet the evolving needs of financial institutions in a rapidly changing regulatory landscape. (Page6)
- It introduces tools for compliance with AMLO (Cap. 615) requirements, including CDD, KYC processes, and screening for risks related to national security, criminal activities, and political exposure. (Page6)

### Acceptance Criteria
- The platform should support screening against national and international watchlists, sanction lists, and PEPs (Page6)
- The platform should facilitate monitoring of financial transactions for suspicious activity (Page6)
- The platform should provide a holistic view of customer data for compliance requirements (Page6)
- The platform should allow searching and analyzing ownership and organizational structures (Page6)

## Functional Requirements for OKP Group Management System

### OKP Group ID Maintenance (Page7)
- **Business Rules**:
  - Groups customer's internal No. for CDD case enquiry/allocation
  - Ensures same maker/checker handle cases (consistency & efficiency)
  - Applies to all customer types: Individual/Non-Individual/PB variants/FI
- **Acceptance Criteria**:
  - System must support customer No. grouping for CDD operations
  - Maintain consistent maker-checker assignment
  - Cover all specified customer types

### OKP Group Management (Page8)
- **Business Rules**:
  - GAOD Admin can create/delete groups and manage members
  - System displays OKP cases in Ongoing CDD enquiry
  - Supports search by OKP Group name
  - Auto-assigns same maker in case creation
  - Verifies group membership during auto-allocation
- **Acceptance Criteria**:
  - Successful group creation/deletion by Admin
  - Correct member addition/removal functionality
  - Accurate display of OKP cases
  - Valid search results by group name
  - Proper maker auto-assignment
  - Correct group member verification

### Add Group Functionality (Page9)
- **Business Rules**:
  - Mandatory group name specification
  - Automatic/manual date capture (DD-MMM-YYYY format)
  - Member addition via Customer Internal Number
- **Acceptance Criteria**:
  - New group creation with valid name
  - Proper date format display
  - Successful member addition by Internal No.
  - Member details display (name, addition date)
